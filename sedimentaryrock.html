 Sedimentary rocks are formed from the accumulation and lithification (compaction and cementation) of sediments. These sediments can come from various sources, leading to different types of sedimentary rocks.

Sedimentary rocks are clastic when they form from the lithification of rocks and mineral fragments such I as quartz, feldspar, and clay. Sedimentary rocks are crystalline when they precipitate out of solution which forms dolomite, calcite, halite or gypsum. Sedimentary rocks can also be bioclastic when it forms as the result of the accumulation of organic material or biological activity. It may even contain remnants of plants, corals, shell, or fossil fragments.

Sedimentary clastic texture may further may refined whether shapes of the individual grains are angular or rounded.
Clasts may also be described based on their sizes and is divided into three types:
  • clay or silt (1/16 mm)
  • Sand (1/16 and 2 mm)
  • Gravel (greater than 2 mm).
